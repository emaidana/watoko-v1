Partner-AI Infrastructure

How partners become native to the system

Most software companies sell partners AI. Watoko becomes their distribution. Any qualified partner — a bank, an insurer, a logistics provider, an accounting firm, a legal firm — plugs into a five-layer infrastructure that turns a legacy service into an autonomous agent, farmer-facing, language-native, A2A-connected, and compounding with every other service on the platform.

1 · Agent Wrapper

Every partner service is wrapped as an autonomous agent with its own tools, memory, and policies. The partner keeps their core system. We turn it into something a farmer can talk to in Swahili over WhatsApp — or something another agent can call programmatically.

2 · Shared Context Graph

Every agent reads from and writes to the same living dataset — farmer identity, farm telemetry, transaction history, biological observations. The insurance agent sees what the credit agent saw. The accounting agent sees what the logistics agent saw. Context stops being a per-vendor privilege.

3 · A2A Protocol

Agent-to-agent messaging is the default, not the exception. When a trade settles on Market, the Payments Agent calls the Accounting Agent calls the Credit Agent calls the Insurance Agent — autonomously, in milliseconds, without a human orchestrator. Partners arrive as islands. They leave as a mesh.

4 · Distribution Layer

Watoko brings the relationship. A farmer who already trusts Brain for planting advice meets their new insurer through the same interface, in the same language, on the same phone. Partners reach smallholders and cooperatives they would never have reached alone, through a channel they wouldn't have known how to build.

5 · Revenue Share + Observability

Every partner shares in the upside they help create — not as a license fee, but as a percentage of activity they caused. The Partner Quality Agent monitors SLA, fee drift, default rates, and farmer outcomes continuously. Partners who raise the quality of the network keep more of the upside. Partners who don't get flagged, re-scoped, or replaced.

The compounding result

A bank that integrates with Ecosystem doesn't just get access to new customers — it inherits the entire intelligence stack. Its credit decisions are sharper because Brain's biological telemetry feeds them. Its default rates drop because Labs' climate models de-risk them. Its growth is priced in farmer outcomes, not origination volume. The partner and the platform get better together, on every cycle.
